Week 8 - Festival of Economics

This week we attended talks at the Festival of Economics. We were tasked with creating 2 graphs which support/oppose an idea presented at a talk we attended. In the 'Big Data' talk, Arthur Turrell (Dep. Director at ONS Data Science Campus) said that in the recent inflation crisis, prices of low cost goods were increasing faster than general prices. All the data used here was taken from ONS supermarket website scraping. My Google Colab notebook shows how i converted this data into TIDY format.

Comparison of Food Price Changes

This chart shows the percentage change in average prices of a range of items from
Sept 2021 - Sept 2022. We can see that typically lower cost 'essential' goods like
vegetable oil, bread, and pasta increased the most in price, supporting Arthur's argument.

Low-Cost Food Items vs CPIH

When we instead compare a basket of lower cost grocery goods
with the CPIH index (an extended measure of CPI including housing-related
costs), we see that this basket did not increase much faster than CPIH.
